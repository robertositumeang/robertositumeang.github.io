Predicting Customer Spending: A Data-Driven Approach

1. Project Overview

This project analyzes customer spending behavior using correlation analysis and multiple linear regression. The goal is to identify key factors influencing yearly spending and provide actionable recommendations for business growth.

2. Project Objective
  • Determine the strongest predictors of yearly spending.
  • Develop a regression model to forecast customer spending.
  • Provide data-driven insights to improve marketing and retention strategies.
3. Tools
  • Excel / Google Sheets – Data cleaning, correlation analysis, regression modeling.
  • Correlation Matrix – Identifying relationships between variables.
  • Multiple Linear Regression – Building a predictive model.
4. Learnings or Results
  • Avg. Session Time & Total Promo have the strongest positive correlation with yearly spending.
  • Bounce Rate negatively impacts spending—higher bounce rates lead to lower revenue.
  • Final Regression Model (93% Accuracy): Yearly Spending = 2,590,241 + (0.0368 × Income) + (64,576 × Tenure) + (6,342 × Avg. Session Time) + (136,148 × Total Promo) - (49,209 × Bounce Rate)
  • Business Recommendations:Increase customer engagement, optimize promotions, and reduce bounce rate for higher spending.
5. Methodology
  • Data Preprocessing – Defining data types, handling missing values.
  • Correlation Analysis – Identifying strong relationships between spending and other factors.
  • Regression Modeling – Building a predictive equation for yearly spending.
  • Model Optimization – Removing insignificant variables to improve accuracy.
  • Prediction – Estimating yearly spending for a new customer using the model.

Optimizing E-Commerce Twin-Date Promotions with Data Analytics

1. Project Overview

This project analyzes twin-date promotional campaigns (10/10, 11/11, and 12/12) for TokoBli, an e-commerce platform. By evaluating past campaign performance, the goal is to identify the most cost-effective strategy that maximizes revenue while minimizing costs.

2. Project Objective
  • Identify the best-performing campaign strategy using historical data.
  • Enhance Return on Investment (ROI) by optimizing discount strategies.
  • Provide data-driven recommendations for future promotional campaigns.
3. Tools
  • Excel / Google Sheets – Data cleaning, statistical analysis, and visualization.
  • Descriptive Statistics – Mean, standard deviation, skewness, kurtosis.
  • EQuartile & Outlier Analysis – IQR method to filter anomalies.
4. Learnings or Results
  • Campaign 11/11 outperformed others with the highest revenue and a balanced discount strategy.
  • Campaign 12/12 had stable sales but lower revenue due to a conservative discount approach.
  • Campaign 10/10 showed high variability, with some high-revenue outliers but inconsistencies in performance.
  • Conclusion:A balanced discount strategy (as seen in 11/11) drives stable customer engagement and optimal revenue.
5. Methodology
  • Data Cleaning – Removing duplicates, handling missing values, and correcting data types.
  • Data Segmentation – Separating data by campaign for accurate comparison.
  • Outlier Detection – Identifying revenue anomalies using quartile analysis.
  • Statistical Analysis – Evaluating campaign performance based on sales quantity, discount strategy, and revenue.
  • Performance Comparison – Identifying trends and selecting the best promotional strategy.
